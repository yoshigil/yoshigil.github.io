Research Topic

Fluid Mechanics

Fluid mechanics underpins nearly all of the geophysical flows I study — from turbulent volcanic jets to dense debris avalanches. I develop theoretical and experimental frameworks to understand how particles, gases, and liquids interact across the wide range of scales encountered in natural hazard systems.

Multiphase Flow Turbulent Jets Entrainment Buoyancy Computational Fluid Dynamics
Motivation

Key research questions

  • How does the presence of dense particles modify the entrainment and buoyancy of turbulent jets?
  • What dimensionless parameters govern the transition between column collapse and sustained convection?
  • How well do Eulerian–Eulerian CFD models capture polydisperse multiphase flows at geophysical scales?
Approach

Methods & data

I use scaled saltwater analog experiments, high-speed imaging, and open-source multiphase CFD solvers to probe the governing physics of particle-laden flows.

Research Topic

Landslides

Landslides and debris flows are among the most hazardous geophysical mass movements, capable of traveling long distances at high velocities. I investigate the rheological and fluid-mechanical controls on runout behavior to improve hazard assessment and early warning capabilities.

Debris Flows Mass Wasting Runout Rheology Hazard Assessment
Motivation

Key research questions

  • What physical mechanisms allow large landslides to travel anomalously long distances?
  • How does the grain size distribution of a debris flow affect its rheological behavior and runout?
  • Can the fluid mechanics of volcanic mass flows inform models for non-volcanic debris avalanches?
Approach

Methods & data

I draw on field mapping, remote sensing data, and depth-averaged flow models to characterize landslide source conditions and constrain deposit volumes and runout distances.
